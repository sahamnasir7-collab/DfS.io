Why choose DfS

Beyond the audit approach

The audit approach increasingly finds itself ineffective in helping achieve social, economic and environmental sustainability goals — a problem Pakistani companies share. Audits focus on forcing compliance rather than owning the process that drives sustainability. That gap is where the need for an alternative was felt.

GIZ introduced Dialogue for Sustainability (DfS): a process-consultation approach designed to bring together representatives throughout the organisational hierarchy, working as a Change Management Team (CMT) toward the common goal of sustainability in day-to-day operations.

How DfS works

From baseline to endline

1

Baseline / GAP analysis

Gives a true picture of existing practices and identifies gaps against best practices.

2

Form the CMT

The partner and its C-suite assemble a Change Management Team with representation from all organisational tiers.

3

CMT workshops & mini-CMTs

Two-day thematic workshops — day one on value-added trainings, day two on the six-step approach. Regular site visits monitor implementation on the shop floor.

4

Endline assessment

Once action-plan items are addressed, the engagement closes with a third-party endline assessment.

Typical engagement: 6–18 months
